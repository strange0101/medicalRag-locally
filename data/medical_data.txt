Malaria is caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes. Symptoms include fever, chills, sweating, headache, and nausea. Treatments include chloroquine and artemisinin-based combination therapies.
Diabetes mellitus (Type 2) is a chronic disease characterized by high blood sugar. Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision. Treatments include insulin, metformin, diet, and exercise.
Tuberculosis is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Symptoms include persistent cough, chest pain, weight loss, night sweats. Treatments include isoniazid, rifampicin, ethambutol, and pyrazinamide.
Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a persistent elevation of blood pressure. Symptoms include headaches and dizziness; often asymptomatic. Treatments include lifestyle changes, ACE inhibitors, beta blockers, diuretics.
COVID-19 is caused by SARS-CoV-2. Symptoms include fever, cough, loss of taste or smell, shortness of breath. Treatments include supportive care, antiviral drugs in severe cases, and preventive vaccines.
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways. Symptoms include wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing. Treatments include inhaled corticosteroids and bronchodilators.
Pneumonia is an infection of the lungs causing cough, fever, and difficulty breathing. Treatments include antibiotics (if bacterial), oxygen therapy, and supportive care.
Migraine is a neurological condition causing severe headache, often with nausea and light sensitivity. Treatments include analgesics, triptans, and lifestyle triggers management.
